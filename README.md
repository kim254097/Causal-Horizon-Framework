# Horizon-Centered Cosmological Program

This repository contains a set of conceptual works exploring horizon-centered approaches in cosmology and gravitational physics.

The common theme across all documents is the structural role of:

- causal accessibility
- horizon-defined domains
- boundary-associated entropy

The project does not introduce new fundamental dynamical laws, modifications of General Relativity, or observational predictions. Instead, it investigates whether established cosmological and gravitational phenomena may admit alternative conceptual organization around causal structure and horizons.

The repository should be understood as a conceptual research program rather than as a completed physical theory.

---

# Scope

The program is:

- conceptual
- non-technical at its foundational level
- compatible with established phenomenology
- agnostic regarding microscopic mechanisms
- intentionally open-ended

The program is not:

- a replacement for ΛCDM
- a modification of General Relativity
- a quantum gravity theory
- a predictive cosmological model
- a unifying framework

No claims of observational discrepancy are made.

# Structural Organization

Documents are in English (`EN/`) and Spanish (`ES/`) subdirectories within each folder. The English version is the primary reference.

## 1. Positioning

`positioning/EN/On_the_Scope_and_Intent_of_Horizon_Centered_Approaches.md`

Primary entry point. Defines scope, intended interpretation, evaluation criteria, and limits of claims. Read this first.

---

## 2. Core Framework

`core/EN/A_Causal–Horizon_Perspective_on_the_Early_Universe.md`

Introduces a horizon-centered interpretation of early-universe cosmology. The observable universe is treated as a causally bounded domain. Operates at the interpretative level and establishes structural consistency principles.

---

## 3. Ontological Layer

`ontology/EN/Ontology_Of_Causally_Self-consistent_Regions.md`

Introduces causally self-consistent regions (CSCRs) as minimal ontological structures compatible with horizon-centered descriptions.

---

## 4. Conceptual Scaffold

`scaffold/EN/Causal_Horizons_As_A_Conceptual_Scaffold_In_Gravitational_And_Cosmological_Physics.md`

Identifies recurring structural principles across horizon thermodynamics, holographic viewpoints, and causal-structure approaches. Organizational — does not imply unification.

---

## 5. Formalization

`formalization/EN/A_Horizon–Centered_Formalization_of_Cosmological_Dynamics.md`

The main technical document. Recovers Friedmann dynamics from horizon thermodynamics and develops the perturbation pipeline linking horizon fluctuations to the primordial power spectrum.

# Evaluation Criteria

The program is intended to be evaluated according to:

- internal conceptual coherence
- structural consistency
- compatibility with established phenomenology
- interpretative usefulness

Absence of microscopic mechanisms or predictive structure should not be interpreted as inconsistency.

---

# Current Status

Work in progress.

- microscopic horizon structure
- boundary-to-bulk mapping
- emergence of geometry
- effective realizations
- relation to quantum gravity

These open questions define future research directions.
